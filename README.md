# Project Management = Construction Practices 2
Throughout their careers, technicians and technologists will be involved in many projects. Students are introduced to the fundamental principles necessary for the successful management of any project. Through simulation and real project planning, students develop skills in creating proposals, budgets, risk assessments, Gantt charts, critical path reports, progress reports, and other industry standard planning and reporting techniques.

# Intro to 3D CAD Drafting = Intro to Civil 3D
Students develop introductory 3D computer-aided design (CAD) skills and produce land-development drawings using current software in the construction industry. Students focus on drawing skills and common standards in consulting and designing offices in order to develop the 3D CAD skills necessary for drawing development, plans review, and construction estimation.

# Materials And Methods
Students analyze materials and methods commonly used or seen in construction associated with architecture and infrastructure development. Also included are common calculations and for various types of civil construction projects.

# Strength Of Materials = Physics 3
In this course, students are introduced to the internal stresses and strains developed in engineering materials when externally loaded. Stresses, and resultant strains, studied include axial stress, shear stress, bending stress, and torsion. Bearing stress and stresses on oblique planes are also covered.
